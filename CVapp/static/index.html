TECHNIQUE

There are three simple rules that will turn your walk into POWER-WALK

90°
Make a 90° angle in your elbows, and swing your arms
from your chest to your hip. Keep your fists loosely closed
heel-to-toe
Make short strides while raising your toes a little to land on the heel,
roll on the ball of the foot, and push the ground with your toes
Posture
Stand tall, pull your shoulders back.
Hold your abs firm and lean a little bit forward

GEAR

Eager to dive in? Just get out and Power-Walk.
To maximize effectiveness and FUN consider these

Walking shoes

Don't settle for anything less then "like a glove". A pair of decent running shoes will do just fine.

Music

Everyone has a music player in his pocket. Rhythmic music will pace you and keep you going and going.

Running tights

You are going to move your feet at a higher then usual pace. Protect yourself from CHAFE!

Heartrate monitor

Speed doesn't matter. What metters is to stay in your zone. What you can't measure, you can't manage.

TIPS

These might help you kick off

For how long should you walk?

This issue depends on your current fitness level. For people that feel they are out of shape, the absolute minimum recommended is 20 minutes. A beginner should start with 25-30 minutes walks. After you are back in shape, you will feel that 30 minutes is just not enough. Then, climb to 40-45 minutes a walk -there's no need going beyond that. The rule of thumb is to increase your workout time by 10 percent a week.

When should I inhale/exhale?

This is an important subject; however there is no single correct answer. In one article I read that you should inhale for three steps and then exhale for three steps. Another article claimed that it should be two and two. I think there is a better approach. Since Power Walking is low-impact and you are supposed to "glide" over the ground, there is much less significance to the breathing rate than there is in running or jogging, where the steps provide the rhythm to the whole body. In Power Walking I found out that the rhythm I'm more drawn to is the rhythm of my music – that's why I choose music with relatively fast beats. To sum up, since your steps have low impact on your body, breathe however feels good to you; whether it’s two-in two-out, three-in three-out, to the music or just natural breathing.

Water during the walk

If you drink enough water throughout the day and before the walk, there is absolutely no need to carry a bottle of water to the walk (it's only 40 minutes outside of home).

Using weights

I didn't see any article that advocates the usage of weights while Power Walking. I've never used weights and my opinion is that if you’re doing it right, Power Walking by itself is enough. However, if you really want it, you can try it and see if that's something that works for you.

Walking alone/in pairs/groups

This is a personal issue with both advantages and disadvantages for either way. I perceive Power Walking to be something I do all by myself; this gives me the chance to get the "meditational walk" and the freedom to do it whenever I want without being dependent on anyone else. This is what works for me. However, if going out for the walk with your friend/friends is what gets you all motivated then you should stick together at least for the first period.
